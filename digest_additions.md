# Digest Additions — Jorge's Japan Trip Apr 13-21
_Added by Hermes: 2026-04-04_

## Tokyo: Shimokitazawa (Vintage Shopping)
Best vintage neighborhood in Tokyo. 10-15 min from Shibuya on Odakyu/Keio Inokashira line.

1. **Haight & Ashbury**
   - **Type**: shop | **Rec**: 9/10
   - Multi-floor vintage with excellent American workwear, denim, and military surplus. Curated but not overpriced.
   - 📍 Near south exit

2. **Flamingo**
   - **Type**: shop | **Rec**: 8/10
   - Chain with good rotation. Women's and men's. Look for the pink signage.

3. **Stick Out**
   - **Type**: shop | **Rec**: 8/10
   - Smaller, more curated. Good for rare finds, band tees.

4. **New York Joe Exchange**
   - **Type**: shop | **Rec**: 7/10
   - Budget-friendly, high volume. Wednesday is ¥500 day.

**Tips:**
- Go on a weekday morning — shops open ~12:00, peak crowds after 15:00
- South side has more shops than north
- Combine with Shimokitazawa cafes and live music venues

## Tokyo: Daikanyama (Cafes + Design)
Tokyo's stylish, quiet neighborhood. 5 min from Shibuya on Tokyu Toyoko line.

1. **T-Site / Tsutaya Books**
   - **Type**: bookstore/cafe | **Rec**: 9/10
   - Stunning architecture (Klein Dytham). Design/art books, lounge with Starbucks, vinyl section. The most beautiful bookstore in Tokyo.

2. **Ivy Place**
   - **Type**: cafe/restaurant | **Rec**: 8/10
   - Inside T-Site complex. Good brunch, outdoor terrace.

3. **Sign Daikanyama**
   - **Type**: cafe | **Rec**: 8/10
   - Minimalist cafe near station. Good for working.

4. **Okura**
   - **Type**: shop | **Rec**: 8/10
   - Indigo dye specialists. Beautiful traditional Japanese textiles and clothing.

**Tips:**
- Perfect rainy day fallback
- Combine with Nakameguro (one stop south) for canal walk + cafes

## Tokyo: Jimbocho (Bookstores)
World's largest used book district. 130+ bookshops. Mita/Hanzomon/Shinjuku lines.

1. **Kitazawa Bookstore (北沢書店)**
   - **Type**: shop | **Rec**: 9/10
   - English books, foreign literature. Multiple floors.

2. **Ohya Shobo (大屋書房)**
   - **Type**: shop | **Rec**: 9/10
   - Ukiyo-e prints, antique maps. Museum-quality pieces.

3. **Isseido (一誠堂書店)**
   - **Type**: shop | **Rec**: 8/10
   - Old building, academic books, first editions.

4. **Komiyama Tokyo**
   - **Type**: shop | **Rec**: 8/10
   - Photo books, art monographs, exhibition catalogs.

**Tips:**
- Most shops close by 18:00
- Jimbocho = curry district too — try Ethiopian (エチオピア) or Bondy for Japanese-style curry

## Takamatsu: Naoshima Art Island
The Benesse Art Site. Ferry from Takamatsu (50 min) or Uno (20 min).

1. **Chichu Art Museum (地中美術館)**
   - **Type**: museum | **Rec**: 10/10
   - Tadao Ando concrete buried in hillside. Monet Water Lilies room, James Turrell light installation, Walter De Maria sphere. Booking required.
   - 📍 South side of island

2. **Benesse House Museum**
   - **Type**: museum | **Rec**: 9/10
   - Contemporary art hotel + museum. Can visit museum without staying. Outdoor sculptures.

3. **Art House Project**
   - **Type**: art | **Rec**: 9/10
   - 7 old houses converted to art installations across Honmura village. Get the multi-site pass. Minami-dera (James Turrell darkness room) is unforgettable.

4. **Yayoi Kusama Pumpkin**
   - **Type**: landmark | **Rec**: 8/10
   - Yellow polka-dot pumpkin on pier. Iconic photo spot. The red one at Benesse House is better (less crowded).

5. **I Love Yu Bathhouse (直島銭湯)**
   - **Type**: activity | **Rec**: 8/10
   - Working bathhouse designed by Shinro Ohtake. Actually bathe in art.

**Tips:**
- Book Chichu Museum tickets online in advance (often sells out)
- Rent electric bike at port — island is hilly
- Full day minimum, ideally overnight at Benesse House
- Many sites closed Mondays

## April 13-21 Timing Notes

**Cherry Blossoms:**
- Kyoto: Past peak by Apr 13 but late bloomers at Ninnaji (Omuro cherries) and Hirano Shrine may still have some
- Shikoku: Slightly later than Kyoto, may catch tail end
- Tokyo: Done by mid-April unless late season

**Weather:**
- Expect 15-22°C, occasional rain
- Pack light layers + compact umbrella

**Crowds:**
- Apr 13-21 is BETWEEN peak seasons (cherry blossom rush ends ~Apr 10, Golden Week starts Apr 29)
- This is actually excellent timing — lower crowds, good weather
- Weekends still busy at major spots

**Golden Week Warning:**
- If extending trip past Apr 27, book EVERYTHING now — trains, hotels triple price and sell out

## Logistics

**JR Pass vs Regional:**
- For Shikoku-focused trip: ALL SHIKOKU Rail Pass (3/4/5/7 days) is better value than full JR Pass
- Covers JR Shikoku trains, some buses
- Buy at Takamatsu, Matsuyama, or Tokushima station

**IC Cards:**
- Get Suica or ICOCA at airport
- Works everywhere except some rural Shikoku buses

**Luggage Forwarding:**
- Use Yamato (Kuroneko) or Sagawa to ship bags between hotels
- ¥2000-3000 per bag, next-day delivery
- Hotels handle drop-off/pickup
- Essential for Shimanami Kaido cycling days
